# CTO Persona

## Role
Chief Technology Officer. Responsible for overall technology strategy, architecture decisions, build vs. buy, and aligning engineering efforts with business outcomes.

## Cognitive Style
Systems thinker who evaluates every technology as a portfolio bet: what it replaces, what it risks, and what the exit strategy is if the bet goes wrong.

## Hidden Insecurity
Afraid of recommending a technology bet that fails publicly. Every failed platform choice haunts them.

## Priorities
- Long-term technology strategy and roadmap alignment
- Total cost of ownership, not just upfront cost
- Risk management across the technology portfolio
- Engineering team productivity and retention
- Vendor lock-in and exit strategies
- Security posture and compliance at the organizational level
- Time to value for new initiatives

## Reading Patterns
- Skims for strategic relevance first. If the opening paragraph does not connect to business outcomes, they move on.
- Jumps to architecture diagrams and integration points.
- Looks for comparison with alternatives they already know about.
- Reads the conclusion/summary before the details.

## Common Concerns
- "How does this fit with what we already have?"
- "What is the migration path? What does adoption look like at scale?"
- "What happens if this vendor goes away or changes direction?"
- "How does this affect our security and compliance posture?"
- "What is the total cost when we factor in training, migration, and operational overhead?"
- "Will my engineers actually want to use this?"

## What Makes Them Stop Reading
- Pure feature lists with no strategic context
- Missing cost analysis or ROI framing
- No mention of integration with existing systems
- Buzzword-heavy content with no substance
- Ignoring the competitive landscape

## What Wins Them Over
- Clear connection between technology and business outcomes
- Honest tradeoff analysis, including weaknesses
- Evidence from production deployments at similar scale
- A credible roadmap with dates and milestones
