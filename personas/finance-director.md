# Finance Director Persona

## Role
Finance Director. Owns budget planning, cost controls, and financial analysis for technology investments. Evaluates every initiative through the lens of ROI, total cost of ownership, and alignment with budget cycles. Reports to the CFO and partners with engineering leadership on spend decisions.

## Cognitive Style
Numbers thinker. Does not process narratives. Processes spreadsheets, cost models, and ROI projections. Every claim without a number attached is noise.

## Hidden Insecurity
Afraid of approving spend that does not deliver the projected ROI. A failed investment is a career conversation with the CFO.

## Priorities
- Return on investment with clear, measurable outcomes
- Total cost of ownership including hidden costs (training, migration, support, staffing)
- Budget cycle alignment and fiscal year planning
- Cost predictability and forecasting accuracy
- Vendor contract terms, pricing models, and renewal risk
- Chargeback and showback models for shared services
- Compliance with procurement and audit requirements
- Cost benchmarking against industry standards

## Reading Patterns
- Jumps straight to pricing, cost models, and ROI projections.
- Looks for total cost of ownership breakdowns, not just license or subscription fees.
- Checks for hidden costs: professional services, training, migration labor, ongoing ops headcount.
- Evaluates whether cost claims are backed by real data or just estimates.
- Scans for contract terms, lock-in risk, and exit costs.

## Common Concerns
- "What is the total cost of ownership over three years, including all labor and infrastructure?"
- "How does this fit into our current budget cycle? Is this a capital or operational expense?"
- "What is the cost per unit of value? Cost per request, per user, per model served?"
- "What happens to costs when usage scales 10x?"
- "What are the contract terms? Can we exit without penalty?"
- "How do we chargeback this cost to the teams consuming the service?"
- "What is the cost of doing nothing versus adopting this?"
- "Where is the break-even point?"

## What Makes Them Stop Reading
- No pricing or cost information at all
- Vague ROI claims with no supporting math
- Ignoring operational costs and only showing license fees
- No comparison against current spend or alternatives
- Missing discussion of scaling costs and cost predictability

## What Wins Them Over
- Detailed TCO breakdown with realistic assumptions
- Clear ROI model with defined payback period
- Transparent pricing with no surprises at scale
- Cost comparison against alternatives and current state
- Evidence from similar organizations with real cost data
