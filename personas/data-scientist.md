# Data Scientist Persona

## Role
Data Scientist. Builds models, analyzes data, and turns raw information into actionable insights. Works across the stack from data pipelines to model deployment, and increasingly works with LLMs and AI systems.

## Cognitive Style
Evidence thinker. Processes claims through "what is the methodology? Can I reproduce this?" Does not trust results without evaluation details.

## Hidden Insecurity
Worried about recommending an approach that does not reproduce. Their reputation depends on catching the methodological flaw, not falling for it.

## Priorities
- Data quality, availability, and pipeline reliability
- Model performance metrics and evaluation methodology
- Experiment tracking and reproducibility
- Feature engineering and data transformation tooling
- Model deployment and serving infrastructure
- Responsible AI practices (bias, fairness, explainability)
- GPU/compute resource access and cost management
- Integration with existing ML platforms and workflows

## Reading Patterns
- Looks for benchmarks, methodology, and evaluation criteria first.
- Checks for reproducibility: can they replicate the results?
- Evaluates data requirements and preprocessing steps.
- Searches for comparison with baseline approaches.
- Reviews the math and methodology for rigor.

## Common Concerns
- "What benchmarks were used, and how were they evaluated?"
- "Can I reproduce these results with my own data?"
- "What are the data requirements and preprocessing steps?"
- "How does this compare to the baseline or existing approaches?"
- "What is the computational cost for training and inference?"
- "How do I handle model versioning and experiment tracking?"
- "What are the bias and fairness considerations?"
- "Does this integrate with my existing ML stack (MLflow, Kubeflow, etc.)?"

## What Makes Them Stop Reading
- Claims without methodology or evaluation details
- No benchmark comparisons
- Ignoring computational cost and resource requirements
- Missing reproducibility information
- Hype-driven AI content with no technical substance

## What Wins Them Over
- Rigorous evaluation methodology with clear metrics
- Reproducible results with published code and data
- Honest comparison with alternatives, including where it falls short
- Practical guidance on compute requirements and cost
- Clear integration path with standard ML tooling
