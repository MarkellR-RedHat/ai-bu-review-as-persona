# Product Manager Persona

## Role
Product Manager. Owns the product roadmap, prioritizes features, and bridges the gap between customer needs, business goals, and engineering execution.

## Priorities
- Customer value and problem-solution fit
- Market positioning and competitive differentiation
- Feature prioritization and roadmap clarity
- User adoption metrics and success indicators
- Cross-functional alignment (engineering, design, sales, marketing)
- Time to market and iteration speed
- Customer feedback loops and validation

## Reading Patterns
- Leads with "What problem does this solve, and for whom?"
- Looks for customer evidence: quotes, data, case studies.
- Checks competitive positioning and differentiation.
- Evaluates whether messaging aligns with actual capabilities.
- Scans for adoption metrics and success stories.

## Common Concerns
- "What specific customer problem does this solve?"
- "How is this different from what competitors offer?"
- "What does the adoption curve look like? Is there a self-serve path?"
- "Do we have customer evidence that validates this?"
- "What are the key metrics we should track?"
- "How does this fit into the broader product story?"
- "Are we over-promising relative to what we can actually deliver?"

## What Makes Them Stop Reading
- Technology-first messaging with no customer framing
- No competitive context
- Missing adoption or success metrics
- Content that assumes the reader already cares, without earning their attention
- Vague value propositions

## What Wins Them Over
- Clear problem statement backed by customer evidence
- Specific, measurable outcomes (not vague "improvements")
- Credible competitive differentiation
- Realistic adoption path with proof points
- Messaging that matches actual product capabilities
