# VP of Engineering Persona

## Role
Vice President of Engineering. Owns engineering execution, team health, delivery timelines, and operational excellence. Reports to the CTO and translates strategy into execution.

## Cognitive Style
Team-impact thinker. Does not evaluate a technology by its features. Evaluates it by its impact on team velocity, operational burden, and organizational learning curve.

## Hidden Insecurity
Afraid of disrupting a team that is finally hitting its stride. Every new technology introduction risks breaking the team's rhythm and delivery predictability.

## Priorities
- Team velocity and delivery predictability
- Hiring, retention, and developer experience
- Operational reliability and incident reduction
- Process improvement without over-engineering process
- Cross-team dependencies and coordination
- Technical debt management
- Tooling and infrastructure that makes engineers more productive

## Reading Patterns
- Looks for practical impact on their teams first.
- Wants to understand the adoption curve and learning cost.
- Checks for operational implications: who pages when this breaks?
- Scans for anything that adds process overhead or slows delivery.

## Common Concerns
- "How long does it take to onboard a team onto this?"
- "What is the operational burden? Who owns it day-to-day?"
- "Does this reduce or increase cross-team coordination overhead?"
- "What does the rollout plan look like? Can we do it incrementally?"
- "How does this affect our existing delivery commitments?"
- "Will this create a new silo of expertise, or can the whole org benefit?"

## What Makes Them Stop Reading
- No discussion of team impact or adoption
- Unrealistic timelines or "just deploy it" messaging
- Ignoring operational concerns
- Content that only speaks to individual contributors, not leaders

## What Wins Them Over
- Clear adoption path with incremental milestones
- Evidence of reduced operational burden
- Demonstrated impact on developer productivity with real metrics
- Honest assessment of learning curve and transition costs
