# Security Architect Persona

## Role
Security Architect. Designs and evaluates the security posture of systems, applications, and infrastructure. Responsible for threat modeling, compliance, and ensuring the organization meets its security obligations.

## Priorities
- Threat modeling and attack surface analysis
- Authentication, authorization, and access control
- Data protection (at rest, in transit, in use)
- Compliance with regulatory frameworks (SOC 2, FedRAMP, HIPAA, PCI-DSS)
- Supply chain security and dependency management
- Secrets management and key rotation
- Audit logging and forensic capabilities
- Zero trust architecture principles

## Reading Patterns
- Immediately looks for the security model and trust boundaries.
- Checks for compliance certifications and audit evidence.
- Evaluates data flow diagrams for exposure points.
- Searches for vulnerability disclosure and patching policies.
- Reviews authentication and authorization mechanisms in detail.

## Common Concerns
- "What is the threat model? What are the trust boundaries?"
- "How is data protected at rest and in transit?"
- "What authentication and authorization mechanisms are in place?"
- "Does this meet our compliance requirements?"
- "What is the vulnerability disclosure and patching process?"
- "How are secrets managed? Is there key rotation?"
- "What does the audit trail look like?"
- "What is the supply chain risk for this dependency?"

## What Makes Them Stop Reading
- No mention of security model or threat analysis
- "We take security seriously" with no specifics
- Missing compliance information
- No discussion of data handling and protection
- Ignoring supply chain concerns

## What Wins Them Over
- Detailed threat model with documented mitigations
- Clear compliance mapping to relevant frameworks
- Transparent vulnerability disclosure process
- Evidence of security testing (pen tests, audits, bug bounties)
- Honest discussion of security tradeoffs and residual risks
