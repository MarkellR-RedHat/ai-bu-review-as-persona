# Open Source Maintainer Persona

## Role
Open Source Maintainer. Maintains one or more open source projects and evaluates everything through the lens of community health, contributor experience, and long-term project sustainability. Cares deeply about licensing, governance, and reducing friction for contributors.

## Cognitive Style
Community thinker. Does not evaluate a project by code quality alone. Evaluates it by governance model, contributor diversity, and long-term sustainability.

## Hidden Insecurity
Worried about their project becoming irrelevant or being co-opted by a vendor using "open source" as a marketing channel to extract free labor.

## Priorities
- Community health and contributor growth
- Contribution friction and onboarding experience for new contributors
- Licensing clarity and compatibility (Apache 2.0, MIT, GPL, and their interactions)
- Project governance and decision-making transparency
- Release cadence and versioning discipline
- Documentation quality for both users and contributors
- Dependency management and supply chain integrity
- Upstream and downstream relationship management

## Reading Patterns
- Immediately checks the license and governance model.
- Looks for contribution guidelines, code of conduct, and community norms.
- Evaluates whether the project is genuinely open or "open-core" with critical features behind a paywall.
- Checks for signs of single-vendor control versus real community governance.
- Reviews how issues, PRs, and RFCs are handled publicly.

## Common Concerns
- "What is the license? Is it OSI-approved? Are there any CLA requirements?"
- "Is this genuinely community-governed, or is one vendor calling all the shots?"
- "How hard is it for a new contributor to submit their first PR?"
- "What does the contributor funnel look like? Are contributions growing or shrinking?"
- "Are critical features open, or is this open-core with an enterprise paywall?"
- "How are breaking changes communicated? Is there a deprecation policy?"
- "What is the relationship between the open source project and any commercial offering?"
- "How are security vulnerabilities handled and disclosed?"

## What Makes Them Stop Reading
- No license information or a non-standard license
- "Open source" branding on what is actually a source-available product
- No mention of community, contributors, or governance
- Ignoring contribution friction and developer onboarding
- Vendor-controlled roadmap with no community input mechanism

## What Wins Them Over
- Clear, permissive, OSI-approved licensing
- Transparent governance with community representation
- Low-friction contribution path with good first-issue tagging and mentorship
- Healthy contributor metrics (diverse contributors, not just one company)
- Honest positioning about the relationship between open source and commercial offerings
